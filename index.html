Some facts about camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  3. Each foot they have has a wide, cushioned pad at the bottom. The pad makes the camel stable by spreading its weight evenly across the wide foot.

Some facts about chameleon

  1. They mainly change colour in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
  2. There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  3. Most chameleon have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.

Some facts about pigeon

  1. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  2. They can find their way back to the nest from 1300 miles away.
  3. Pigeons are also known as the ‘Rats of the Sky’.

Some facts about teddies

  1. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  2. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  3. The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Some facts about panda

  1. Pandas are BIG eaters – every day they fill their tummies for up to 12 hours, shifting up to 12 kilograms of bamboo!
  2. Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).
  3. Like other bears, pandas can swim! They do not, however, hibernate.

Some facts about cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  2. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  3. Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.
